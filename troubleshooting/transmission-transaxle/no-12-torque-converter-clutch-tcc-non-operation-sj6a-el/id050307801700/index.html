NO.12 TORQUE CONVERTER CLUTCH (TCC) NON-OPERATION [SJ6A-EL]

12 }{: #wp1061376

Torque converter clutch (TCC) non-operation {: #wp1059787}

DESCRIPTION {: #wp1059860}

• TCC does not operate when vehicle reaches TCC operation range. {: #wp1059863}

POSSIBLE CAUSE {: #wp1059867}

• Basically, TCC does not operate when the fail-safe is operating. Verify the DTC at first. If the TCC operates when driving at high speeds only, the malfunction (improper adjustment) is in the M range switch circuit or TR switch circuit. {: #wp1059870} Caution } • If the TCC is stuck, inspect it. In addition, inspect the oil cooler for foreign particles which may have mixed in with the ATF. {: #wp1061298} • TCC slippage, burnt {: #wp1059958} - Line pressure low {: #wp1059968} • Incorrect throttle position signal {: #wp1059982}• Incorrect accelerator pedal position signal {: #wp1059996}• Incorrect engine speed signal {: #wp1060006}• Incorrect engine coolant temperature signal {: #wp1060016}• Incorrect engine torque signal {: #wp1060026}• Turbine sensor malfunction {: #wp1060036}• VSS malfunction {: #wp1060050}• Output solenoid valve system malfunction (Sticking) {: #wp1060060}• TCC control solenoid malfunction {: #wp1060070}• Control valve body system malfunction (Poor operation, sticking) {: #wp1060080}• TCC hydraulic pressure system malfunction {: #wp1060090} • Accelerator pedal position sensor malfunction (Not operating linearly) {: #wp1060100} • Turbine sensor or VSS malfunction {: #wp1060110} • Operation of fail-safe function. {: #wp1060120} - Short to power or GND at TCC control solenoid related circuit. {: #wp1060131*Note*{: #wp1060145} • Before following the troubleshooting steps, make sure that the Automatic Transmission On-Board Diagnostic and Automatic Transmission Basic Inspection are conducted. (See BASIC INSPECTION [SJ6A-EL].) {: #wp1061299}

STEP {: #wp1060290}

INSPECTION {: #wp1060292}

ACTION {: #wp1060296}

1 {: #wp1060184}

• With the ignition switch at the ON position. {: #wp1060186} • Does the gear position indicator light indication correspond to the selector lever position? {: #wp1060406}

Yes {: #wp1060242}

Go to the next step. {: #wp1060245}

No {: #wp1060325}

Go to No.27 "GEAR POSITION INDICATOR LIGHT DOES NOT ILLUMINATE IN M RANGE", or No.28 "GEAR POSITION INDICATOR LIGHT ILLUMINATES IN D RANGE OR P, N, R POSITIONS". {: #wp1060328}

2 {: #wp1060332}

• Disconnect the TCM. {: #wp1060335} • Is the resistance between the TCM ground terminal 1P, 1X and the body GND less than 5.0 ohms? {: #wp1060462}

Yes {: #wp1060338}

Go to the next step. {: #wp1060341}

No {: #wp1060351}

Repair open ground circuit. {: #wp1060354}

3 {: #wp1060358}

• Access the following PCM PIDs using the M-MDS. {: #wp1060361} - RPM {: #wp1060539}- APP {: #wp1060553}- TP {: #wp1060563}- ECT {: #wp1060573}- ENG_TRQ {: #wp1060583} • Are PIDs value normal? {: #wp1060595}

Yes {: #wp1060364}

No {: #wp1060505}

4 {: #wp1060623}

Yes {: #wp1060629}

No {: #wp1060644}

• Inspect the TCC control solenoid. {: #wp1060722} (See SOLENOID VALVE INSPECTION [SJ6A-EL].) {: #wp1060781} • Is the line pressure control solenoid normal? {: #wp1060795}

Yes {: #wp1060725}
