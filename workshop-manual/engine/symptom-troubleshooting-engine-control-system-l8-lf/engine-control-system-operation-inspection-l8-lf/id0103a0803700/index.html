Throttle position (TP) sweep inspection

  1. Connect the M-MDS to the DLC-2.

  2. Turn the ignition switch to the ON position.

  3. Verify that none of the following DTC are displayed:

• P0122, P0123, P0222, P0223, P0638, P2101, P2107, P2108, P2109, P2112, P2119, P2122, P2123, P2127, P2128, P2135, P2138

  • If any one DTC is displayed, perform DTC inspection.

  1. Access TP PID.

  2. Verify that the PID reading is within the CTP value. (See PCM INSPECTION [L8, LF].)

• If the PID reading is out of range, perform the following:

  • Remove the air duct from the throttle valve body. {: #wp1060526}- Verify that the throttle valve opens when the accelerator pedal is depressed.

• If the throttle valve opens, inspect the throttle position sensor and related wiring harness. {: #wp1060550}• If the throttle valve does not open, inspect the throttle actuator control motor and related wiring harness.

  1. Gradually depress the throttle pedal and verify that the PID reading increases accordingly.

• If the PID reading drops momentarily, inspect the following:

  • Throttle position sensor
  1. Fully depress the throttle pedal and verify that the PID reading is within WOT value. (See PCM INSPECTION [L8, LF].)

• If the PID reading is out of range, perform the followings:

  • Remove the air duct from throttle valve body. {: #wp1060644}- Verify that the throttle valve opens when throttle pedal is depressed.

• If the throttle valve opens, inspect the throttle position sensor and related wiring harness. {: #wp1060668}• If the throttle valve does not open, inspect the throttle actuator control motor and related wiring harness.

EGR Control System Inspection

  1. Crank the engine and verify that EGR valve operation (initial operation) sound is heard.

• If the operation sound is not heard, connect M-MDS to the DLC-2 and verify that the DTC P0403 is shown. Perform the DTC inspection. (See DTC TABLE [L8, LF].)

  1. Start the engine and run it at idle.

  2. Increase the step value of EGR valve from 0 to 52 using SEGRP PID.

  3. Operate the EGR valve and inspect if the engine speed becomes unstable or the engine stalls.

• If the engine speed will not change, proceed to following. {: #wp1063955}(1) Stop the engine. {: #wp1063969}(2) Remove the EGR valve. {: #wp1063979}(3) Connect the EGR valve connector. {: #wp1063989}(4) Turn the ignition switch to the ON position. {: #wp1063999}(5) Increase the step value of EGR valve from 0 to 52 using SEGRP PID. {: #wp1064029}(6) Inspect the EGR operation.

• If the EGR valve is operated, clean the EGR valve and reinspect from Step 2. {: #wp1064048}• If the EGR valve will not operate, replace the EGR valve and reinspect from Step 2.

  1. Start the engine and warm it up completely.

  2. Access the following PIDs.

• ECT {: #wp1064092}• RPM {: #wp1064106}• SEGRP {: #wp1064116}• TP {: #wp1064126}• VSS

  1. Idle the vehicle and verify that the SEGRP value is 0.

  2. Put the vehicle in drive.

  3. Depress the accelerator pedal and verify that the SEGRP value is increased.

• If the SEGRP value will not increase, inspect the VSS, APP1, APP2, TP and ECT PIDs. (See PCM INSPECTION [L8, LF].)

  1. Stop the vehicle and verify that the SEGRP value is returned 0.

Purge Control System Inspection

  1. Start the engine.

  2. Disconnect the vacuum hose between the purge valve and the charcoal canister.

  3. Put a finger to the purge valve and verify that there is no vacuum applied when the engine is cold.

• If there is a vacuum, inspect the following.

  • Wiring harness and connectors (Purge valve-PCM terminal 2C) {: #wp1064280}- Purge valve

  1. Start the engine and warm it up completely.

  2. Stop the engine.

  3. Connect the M-MDS to the DLC-2 and verify that the DTC P0443 is shown. Perform the DTC inspection. (See DTC TABLE [L8, LF].)

  4. Turn the ignition switch to the ON position.

  5. Access ECT PID.

  6. Verify that the engine coolant temperature is above 78 °C {173 °F}.

• If M-MDS indicates below 78 °C {173 °F}, perform the ECT sensor inspection.

  1. Access EVAPCP PID.

  2. Increase the duty value of the purge valve to 50% and inspect if the operation sound of the valve is heard.

• If the operation sound is heard, inspect for the loose or damaged vacuum hose. (Intake manifold-purge valve-charcoal canister) {: #wp1064452}• If the operation sound is not heard, perform the purge valve inspection.

Variable Valve Timing Control System Operation Inspection

If simulation function of M-MDS is used:

  1. Warm up the engine to normal operating temperature.

  2. Connect the M-MDS to DLC-2.

  3. Start the engine and run it at idle.

  4. Select VT DUTY1 PID.
